Jeff Koons at the Sheldonian Theatre
5/8/2019 18:00-19:45
Sheldonian Theatre

Contact: Irene Yang

£10

Come join your fellow Clarendons at a fantastic event at the Sheldonian Theatre.

From monumental balloon dogs to subversive large-scale paintings, contemporary artist Jeff Koons is known for pushing the boundaries of art. What is the inspiration behind his bold and provocative works, and what role do they play in the world of contemporary art more broadly?

Find out in this vibrant conversation event, at the Sheldonian Theatre, between Jeff Koons and Emeritus Professor of Art History Martin Kemp.

The Town Hall: Tour
2/16/2019 14:00-15:00
Oxford Town Hall

Contact: Serte Donderwinkel

FREE

The Town Hall is a very special bit of Oxford history. It hosts many facilities that are related to Oxford as a town, instead of just a university. The city council has their meetings there, there is a court of justice, a library, a concert hall, a police station, and much more.

This tour, hosted by the Museum of Oxford, will shine a light on the struggle between Town and Gown from the town perspective. This venue is accessible.

Chinese New Year Festival show at Oxford Playhouse
2/18/2019 19:00-21:00
The Oxford Playhouse

Contact: Irene Yang

£16

Come and join the celebrations as Chinese New Year returns to Oxford Playhouse with some thrilling new acts.

Touring across the country to celebrate the biggest festive event on the Chinese calendar, Chinese New Year Festival Show is a festive spectacle showcasing the best of Chinese performing arts.

From the daring stunts of Chinese acrobatics to the magnificent 18-metre long Dragon Dance, and more, this is a colourful party of Chinese cultural tradition.

Not to be missed.

This venue is accessible.

Tour Fantastic Cities @ Modern Art Oxford
2/9/2019 12:00-13:00
Modern Art Oxford

Contact: Serte Donderwinkel

FREE

Fantastic Cities is an exhibition of the work of Penny Woolcock, who addresses issues of social inequality. Her award-winning work centres on personal stories of how power and influence impact marginalised communities. The artist started her career in Oxford, and her short film 'Dreaming Spires' on inequality in our city is also part of the exhibition.

Sara Lowes, the Curator of Creative Learning of Modern Art Oxford will give an introduction of the work of Woolcock, after which we can explore the exhibition ourselves. This venue is accessible.
