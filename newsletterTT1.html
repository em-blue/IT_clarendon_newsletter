She’s A Good Boy
4/27/2019 19:30
The Old Fire Station, Oxford

Contact: Shiyan Tang

Scholar Ticket: £5
Guest: £10

This is a solo show in which Elise shares their real life experiences of non-binary gender in a striking performance style which verges on stand-up.

Moment from the hilarious to the heartbreaking are recreated using physical performance, live music and comedy songs, to share the challenges of society that is conditioned to see only masculine or feminine.

The story is told with energy throughout-form seahorse to spiderman socks, tributes to disgruntled local councilors and weeding day disasters. An honest, moving and hilarious show exploring gender and identity.

Cotswolds Walking Trip to Moreton-in-Marsh
4/28/2019 9:40-17:00
Oxford Station

Contact: Victoria Pipas

FREE

We are delighted to announce an organized walking trip in the Cotswolds on Sunday, April 28th, providing a refreshing and active way to ring in the new term. The walk will be led by our very own Till Weidner, who is a certified Level 1 Mountain Leader with R.E.C. Level 2 First Aid training (but n.b.: neither Till nor the Council accepts liability for participants on the walk).

We will meet at the Oxford Station at 9:40am to catch the 9:55am train to Kingham, from whence we will begin an ~19.5km walk to Moreton-in-Marsh via Adelstrop. The walk has gentle ascents, passing through several Cotswolds villages, some of which inspired Jane Austen's Mansfield Park.

We will have the option for a lunch break at the Foxx Inn, but it is also recommended that you pack a lunch, snacks, and water, and that you wear a raincoat and proper walking shoes, i.e. hiking boots or water-resistant sneakers. We will finish at a pub in Moreton for a drink, and aim to return to Oxford before 5pm.

Additional information:

1. If you have a railcard, please bring it.
2. Guests are very welcome and encouraged to join! However, note that they will have to pay their own train fares.

Jeff Koons at the Sheldonian Theatre
5/8/2019 18:00-19:45
Sheldonian Theatre

Contact: Irene Yang

SOLD OUT

Come join your fellow Clarendons at a fantastic event at the Sheldonian Theatre.

From monumental balloon dogs to subversive large-scale paintings, contemporary artist Jeff Koons is known for pushing the boundaries of art. What is the inspiration behind his bold and provocative works, and what role do they play in the world of contemporary art more broadly?

Find out in this vibrant conversation event, at the Sheldonian Theatre, between Jeff Koons and Emeritus Professor of Art History Martin Kemp.

Wonderland: Clarendon Garden Ball 2019
6/16/2019 15:00-23:00
Isis River Farmhouse

Contact: Paula Rocktaeschel

FREE

The most anticipated Trinity term event is finally on the horizon! The planning committee presents, Wonderland: The Clarendon Garden Ball 2019.

On June 16th, enter a land that transcends the imagination. Located at the Isis Farmhouse and inspired by a Lewis Carroll classic, a river boat ride will escort you “gently down the stream”, where you will be greeted at this fairy-tale riverside pub by a signature cocktail and hors d'oeuvres.

As the afternoon progresses, aided by our open bar and general madness, enjoy music, entertainment, a barbeque dinner, ice cream and candy bars, open fires, and riverside merriment.



Dress code:
As a “garden ball”, the event is envisioned as a hybrid between the more formal (and expensive) Oxford balls and relaxed summer celebration. We therefore invite you wear forms of fancy dress that accomodate games of flamingoed croquet and river-side dancing.
